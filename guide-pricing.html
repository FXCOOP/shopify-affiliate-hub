Hidden Costs Most People Miss

1. Inventory & Product Costs

  • If dropshipping: $0 upfront (pay per sale)
  • If holding inventory: $500-5,000 initial stock
  • If manufacturing: $2,000-20,000+ for first production run

2. Shipping Supplies

  • Boxes/mailers: $0.50-2.00 per order
  • Packing materials: $0.25-1.00 per order
  • Tape, labels, inserts: $0.10-0.50 per order
  • Total per order: $0.85-3.50

3. Refunds & Chargebacks

  • Industry average: 2-5% of orders get refunded
  • Chargeback fees: $15-25 per dispute
  • Annual impact: $200-1,000 for most stores

4. Photography & Content

  • DIY: $0 (use your phone)
  • Basic equipment: $200-500 (lighting, backdrop)
  • Professional photos: $50-200 per product
  • Product descriptions: $25-100 per product (if outsourced)

How to Minimize Costs

  1. Start with Basic plan: Don't pay for features you don't need yet
  2. Use Shopify Payments: Avoid 2% transaction fees
  3. Free apps first: Only upgrade when you outgrow free plans
  4. Free theme initially: Dawn theme is excellent and free
  5. DIY photos: Phone cameras are good enough to start
  6. Organic marketing: Social media, SEO, content marketing (free)
  7. Dropshipping: No inventory costs upfront
  8. Start lean: Don't buy every app and tool on day one

When to Upgrade Your Plan

Upgrade from Basic to Shopify ($105/month) when:

  • You're consistently doing $10,000+/month in sales
  • The lower credit card fees will save you money (do the math!)
  • You need professional reports for inventory/sales analysis
  • You have 3+ team members who need access

Math example:

  • $10K sales/month with Basic: $10,000 √ó 2.9% = $290 in fees
  • $10K sales/month with Shopify: $10,000 √ó 2.7% = $270 in fees
  • Savings: $20/month... but plan costs $66 more
  • Break-even point: ~$33,000/month in sales

So actually, wait until you're doing $30K+/month to upgrade!
